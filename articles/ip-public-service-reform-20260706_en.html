CNIPA's New Policy: 11 Reforms, 3 Opportunities, 1 Warning


He Zigang | 20-Year IP Attorney | Aipunajie · Mili · Najie

On June 22, 2026, the China National Intellectual Property Administration (CNIPA) issued Document No. 20 (2026) — the "Opinions on Strengthening the Management of IP Public Service Institutions and Delivering High-Quality IP Public Services."


As of today, over 14 days since publication, the document has received zero professional interpretation across the entire industry.


If you work in an IP service institution, this article is for you.



I. Why This Document Is Worth 15 Minutes of Your Time


First, some context.


In August 2024, CNIPA issued the "Guiding Opinions on Comprehensively Improving the Effectiveness of IP Public Services." That was version 1.0 — directional guidance.


The June 2026 Document No. 20 is version 2.0 — concrete action.


Signal 1: From "Encouragement" to "Assessment"

No longer just "encourage construction" — but "hierarchical management, performance evaluation, dynamic rankings." TISC (Technology and Innovation Support Centers) move from certification to ongoing performance review.


Signal 2: From "Passive Waiting" to "Active Delivery"

Public service outlets must extend down to counties, cities, and industrial parks. The SME IP special action program continues to advance. Service institutions no longer wait for clients — they deliver services to the factory floor.


Signal 3: From "Manual Work" to "AI-Powered"

For the first time, policy explicitly encourages development of "small and beautiful" smart tools and service products. AI is not optional — it's policy-directed.



II. Breaking Down the 11 Reforms


I've broken down Document No. 20 into 4 dimensions — read through once and you'll know what to focus on.


Dimension 1: Institutional Management (Articles 1-2)


| Reform | Key Point | Who It Affects | |:-------|:----------|:---------------| | Tiered Management | CNIPA coordinates, provincial bureaus manage locally | All TISC and public service institutions | | Grassroots Coverage | Outlets extend to counties, cities, industrial parks | Firms with regional presence |

Dimension 2: Service Delivery (Articles 3-6)


| Reform | Key Point | Who It Affects | |:-------|:----------|:---------------| | Collaborative Structure | TISC, university service centers, protection centers work together | Large firms with multiple institution types | | SME Focus | Continued SME IP special action program | Firms serving SME clients | | Strategic Industries | Full-cycle services around emerging and future industries | Firms with industry specialization | | Original Innovation | Connect with national labs and research institutions | Firms with research client networks |

Dimension 3: Tools & Technology (Articles 7-10)


| Reform | Key Point | Who It Affects | |:-------|:----------|:---------------| | Industry-Academia | Connect innovation consortia, tech transfer centers | Firms with university channels | | Online-Offline | National integrated service platform, "one-stop processing" | Firms with digital operations | | Data Empowerment | IP big data center construction | All institutions (data is infrastructure) | | AI + New Tech | "Small and beautiful" smart tools encouraged | Every institution — mandatory |

Dimension 4: Evaluation (Article 11)


Evaluation-driven excellence: build benchmark TISCs, promote leadership through demonstration.


In plain language: TISC is no longer a lifetime designation. Poor performance means losing the title.

III. Three Opportunities


Opportunity 1: Government Project Funding


TISC upgrades, grassroots outlet construction, SME special programs — each direction may have corresponding government procurement or special funding.


Action: Watch for provincial implementing policies, typically issued 30-60 days after Document No. 20.

Opportunity 2: AI Tool Track


Policy explicitly encourages "AI + IP" tool development. This is not reserved for tech companies — tools developed by IP service institutions themselves receive priority support.


Action: If you're already working on AI tools (annuity management, trademark monitoring, patent search), now is the optimal window to apply for recognition and support.

Opportunity 3: Data Services


Big data center construction means data opening. Data becomes a new service product — patent data analysis, industry trend reports, competitive intelligence.


Action: Prepare your data product line. Be ready when data becomes available.

IV. One Warning


TISC performance-based elimination is coming.

Not immediate, but the signal is clear — from "once certified, forever secure" to "certified today, must perform tomorrow."


Check your TISC or public service qualifications. How far are you from meeting the "dynamic evaluation" standard?



V. Three Things to Do Right Now


1. Read the original. CNIPA Document No. 20 (2026) is available on the CNIPA website. Don't rely on second-hand interpretations.

2. Watch for provincial policies. Implementing rules will be issued within 30-60 days.

3. Evaluate your AI tools. Can they be positioned as "small and beautiful" smart service products?
